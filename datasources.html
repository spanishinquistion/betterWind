  • Analysis Methods

Our data is provided by the Deutsche WetterDienst (DWD), which has over 400 measurement stations with available data. OpenWind provides the preliminary analysis using hourly measurement data from these sites. We extract three principle data points from their data stream.

Wind Direction

A preliminary analysis of the wind direction is provided along with the prevailing wind direction.

Why this is important: Wind farms can take advantage of prevailing wind directions to optimize layouts.

Years of data Y years

The amount of data available varies at each site. The more data, the more accurate the predictions for future wind conditions. A standard wind site assessment requires at least one year of data at a prospective site. Each site page (Aachen Example) lists how many years of data are available and how recently the data has been updated.

Why this is important: More years of data means more predictable wind generation results.

Average wind speed VAVE m/s

The wind speed average at each site is calculated along with averages based on standard tower heights. The heights of the measurement stations vary according to site and have changed over the years. The change in measurement heights in integrated into all the calculations and noted in the site page. The data is quality checked to remove all missing or bad data and the data quality noted for all sites.

Why this is important: Average wind speed when properly prepared serves as a primary datapoint for calculation of energy production.
